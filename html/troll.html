The Patent Troll

  • Life : 100
  • Attack : 100
  • Defense : 0
  • Special : Lawsuit!
    If enemy has less than 30 Defense, Patent Troll gets +100 Attack

A group of people or a business sometimes referred to as 'aggressive Non-Profit Entities(NPR)' solely bent on making money off of your ideas. They beat you to the patent office and use a general patent description for a product that they don't even intend to create. If your new software comes close to their description, they threaten to sue for patent infringement. You then come to a settlement with them because you don't have enough money to fight them in court. They gladly take your money.

Quick Summary

"In 2011, patent trolls cost U.S. companies more than $29 billion in legal fees and settlement costs, according to a study by the Boston University School of Law. The trolls target mostly small and medium-size businesses--companies with annual revenue of $10.8 million on average, according to the study. Nearly any company that uses basic technology or operates a website is a possible target. Trolls are litigation machines, the natural result of a patent system that has done a terrible job of evaluating and granting software patents--and a court system that hasn't done much better at examining them."

How to fight

If you have the money, fight them in court. Most trolls don't even have a working prototype. They only win a small percentage of court cases.

Always search to make sure that their patent is actually valid.

Combine your resources with other entrepreneurs or companies that are also being attacked by the troll. The more resources you have the better chance you can fight them in court and win.

Make the process as painful as possible for the troll. Their whole ethic system is based upon convenience.
